Research Profile

This project is part of a broader research agenda on organised violence, urban conflict and criminal governance in Latin America.

Academic positioning.

My research focuses on the intersection between organised crime, urban violence and governance, with a particular emphasis on Argentina and the city of Rosario. I am interested in how criminal actors shape urban space, how violence is distributed across territory, and how event data can support qualitative interpretation rather than replace it.

Research contribution.

This portfolio project demonstrates how conflict event-data methods can be adapted to the study of criminal violence in urban settings. By combining structured observations, geospatial visualisation and case-based interpretation, the project positions Rosario as a relevant empirical case for analysing criminal governance, civilian targeting and the spatial organisation of violence.

Research interests.

Urban violence and spatial dynamics

Organised crime and criminal governance

Drug trafficking and transnational networks

Event data and conflict analysis
